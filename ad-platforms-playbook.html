The Complete Paid Traffic Playbook

A step-by-step system for running YouTube, TikTok, and Facebook/Meta ads as one connected funnel — not three disconnected platforms.

Part 1 — The Funnel Strategy

Before opening any ads manager, get this part right. Every platform-specific mistake people make traces back to skipping this step: running ads without a funnel, so every dollar is asked to do the whole job (stranger → buyer) in one impression. Almost nothing converts that way.

1.1 — The three-stage funnel

1
Cold (Awareness)People who don't know you yet. Your job here is NOT to sell — it's to earn 3-10 seconds of genuine attention and get a low-friction action (watch more, click to a free guide, follow). Selling directly to cold traffic is the single biggest reason ad accounts burn money with nothing to show for it.
2
Warm (Consideration)People who engaged with your cold content but haven't bought — video viewers, page visitors, email subscribers, social engagers. This is where you retarget with proof, specifics, and a clear offer.
3
Hot (Conversion)People who showed real purchase intent — added to cart, visited a pricing page, started checkout. Smallest audience, highest intent, should get your strongest direct offer (discount code, urgency, direct CTA).

1.2 — Budget allocation across the funnel

A reasonable starting split while you're still learning what works: 60% Cold / 30% Warm / 10% Hot. As your retargeting audiences fill up and start converting efficiently, you'll often shift toward 40/40/20 — but never starve the top of the funnel completely, or your warm/hot audiences eventually run dry.

1.3 — Tracking, before you spend a single dollar

  • Meta Pixel + Conversions API on every page of your site (especially the checkout/thank-you page) — set up in Meta Events Manager.
  • TikTok Pixel via TikTok Events Manager — same principle, install before launching, not after.
  • Google Tag (for YouTube/Google Ads conversion tracking) via Google Tag Manager or Google Ads' own tag.
  • Confirm every pixel actually fires (use each platform's built-in test/debug tool) before spending real budget — a silently broken pixel is the most common reason "ads don't work" when the real problem is invisible data.

1.4 — The creative testing framework (use this on all three platforms)

Every ad lives or dies on three things, in this order: Hook (0-3 seconds) → Retention (does it keep watching?) → CTA (clear next step). Test hooks first — it's the highest-leverage variable. A mediocre offer with a great hook usually outperforms a great offer with a weak hook.

Always test in batches of 3-5 creative variations per audience, let each get enough impressions to be statistically meaningful (roughly 1,000+ impressions or a few days, whichever comes first) before judging — killing an ad after a few hours is the second most common beginner mistake.

Part 2 — YouTube Ads, Step by Step

2.1 — Pick the right campaign type

TypeBest forSkippable?
Skippable in-streamCold awareness, storytellingYes, after 5s
Bumper (6s)Brand recall, retargetingNo — always plays
Demand Gen (formerly Discovery)Feed-style placements, lower pressureN/A

For most lead-magnet/funnel use cases: start with Skippable in-stream for cold traffic, add a Bumper campaign once you have a retargeting list.

2.2 — Targeting (most important decision on this platform)

1
Custom Intent / Custom SegmentsBuild an audience from search terms and URLs your ideal customer would actively search for or visit. This is YouTube's most powerful cold-targeting lever — more precise than broad demographics.
2
In-Market audiencesPeople Google has already identified as actively researching a purchase in a relevant category.
3
PlacementsTarget specific channels/videos where your audience already watches — slower to set up, often the highest quality traffic once you've found 5-10 good placements.

2.3 — Step-by-step campaign setup

  1. Google Ads → New Campaign → Objective: Leads or Sales (not "Awareness" unless that's genuinely the goal) → Campaign type: Video
  2. Choose Skippable in-stream → set a Target CPA or Maximum CPV bid strategy
  3. Set daily budget — start at an amount you're comfortable losing entirely for 5-7 days while you learn
  4. Build your audience using 2.2 above — start with ONE targeting method per ad group, not stacked, so you can tell what's actually working
  5. Upload 2-3 video variations with different hooks in the first 5 seconds
  6. Launch, then do not touch anything for 48-72 hours — YouTube's algorithm needs a stable learning period

2.4 — Video structure that actually holds attention

0-5s: Hook — a specific claim, question, or visual that stops the scroll/skip. 5-20s: Agitate the problem your audience already feels. 20-45s: Present your solution with one concrete proof point. Final 10s: One clear CTA, said out loud and shown on screen.

2.5 — Weekly optimization cadence

Check daily for the first week (without touching bids/targeting), then move to: weekly — pause anything with view rate well below your other ads, double budget on anything converting at or below your target cost-per-result, refresh creative every 2-3 weeks before "ad fatigue" sets in (rising CPMs, falling CTR on the same creative).

Part 3 — TikTok Ads, Step by Step

3.1 — Spark Ads vs. standard ads

Spark Ads (boosting an existing organic post, yours or a creator's with permission) consistently outperform freshly-uploaded "ad-only" creative on TikTok — they inherit the organic post's social proof (likes/comments) and feel native to the feed. Use Spark Ads as your default whenever you have an organic post that's already showing signs of life.

3.2 — Targeting options

  1. Interest & behavior targeting — broad but useful for initial cold discovery
  2. Custom Audiences — website visitors (via pixel), customer email lists, engagement audiences (people who watched X% of your videos)
  3. Lookalike Audiences — built from your best Custom Audience (e.g., past purchasers); the single highest-leverage TikTok targeting move once you have 100+ purchasers to seed it
TikTok's algorithm is unusually good at finding the right viewer on its own — over-narrowing targeting early often performs worse than starting broader and letting the algorithm's own optimization do the work, especially during the learning phase.

3.3 — Step-by-step campaign setup

  1. TikTok Ads Manager → Campaign → Objective: Conversions (or Traffic if you don't have pixel events set up yet)
  2. Ad group → Placements: Automatic (let it include TikTok + Pangle network initially, narrow later if Pangle underperforms)
  3. Targeting: start broad (3.2 above) — one interest cluster, not five stacked
  4. Budget: Cost Cap or Lowest Cost bidding to start; switch to Cost Cap once you know your target cost-per-result
  5. Creative: native, vertical (9:16), filmed like a real TikTok — not a repurposed TV ad. Text overlays and captions are expected, not optional.
  6. Launch with 3-5 creative variants per ad group, let TikTok's auto-optimization find the winner

3.4 — Creative best practices

  • Hook in the first 1-2 seconds — TikTok's scroll speed is faster than any other platform
  • Native, UGC-style filming (handheld, talking-to-camera) outperforms polished studio production almost every time
  • Lean into current trends/sounds when genuinely relevant — never force it if it doesn't fit the message
  • Captions on always — large portion of viewers watch muted

3.5 — Optimization cadence

TikTok creative fatigues faster than YouTube or Facebook — plan to refresh creative every 1-2 weeks, not monthly. Check cost-per-result daily for the first 3 days of any new ad group, then weekly once stable.

Part 4 — Facebook/Meta Ads Targeting, Step by Step

4.1 — Campaign structure: CBO vs. ABO

Campaign Budget Optimization (CBO)Ad Set Budget Optimization (ABO)
Best forLetting Meta auto-distribute budget across ad setsControlled testing — you decide each ad set's spend
Use whenScaling a proven funnelTesting new audiences/creative

Recommended: ABO while testing (so one lucky/unlucky ad set doesn't starve the others of data), CBO once you've identified 2-3 winning ad sets to scale.

4.2 — The three audience types you actually need

1
Core/Detailed targeting (cold)Interests, demographics, behaviors. Keep it simple — 1-3 tightly related interests per ad set, not a kitchen-sink stack of 20.
2
Custom Audiences (warm)Built from your pixel (website visitors, video viewers, page engagers) or uploaded customer/email lists. This is where your retargeting budget goes.
3
Lookalike Audiences (cold, but smarter)Meta finds people who resemble your best existing Custom Audience (e.g., past purchasers, top 25% by value). Build a 1% Lookalike off your purchaser list as soon as you have at least 100 purchases — usually the single best-performing cold audience on the platform.

4.3 — Retargeting funnel setup

  1. Custom Audience: all website visitors, last 30 days, EXCLUDING purchasers → show proof/testimonials + a direct offer
  2. Custom Audience: video viewers (50%+) who didn't click → show a stronger, more specific CTA than the original ad
  3. Custom Audience: added-to-cart but didn't purchase, last 7 days → show urgency (limited discount, expiring bonus) — your highest-converting, smallest-budget ad set

4.4 — Creative testing with Dynamic Creative

Turn on Dynamic Creative when testing — upload several headlines, images/videos, and primary text variants, and Meta auto-combines and tests them, surfacing the best-performing combination faster than manually testing one-by-one.

4.5 — Step-by-step campaign setup

  1. Meta Ads Manager → Create → Objective: Sales or Leads (matches your actual funnel goal, not "Awareness" or "Engagement" unless that's truly the goal)
  2. Ad set → Conversion location: Website (confirm pixel event matches your actual checkout/lead event)
  3. Audience: pick ONE of the three types from 4.2 per ad set — never mix cold + retargeting logic in the same ad set
  4. Placements: start with Advantage+ (automatic) unless you have a specific reason to manually restrict to certain placements
  5. Budget: ABO while testing (4.1) — enough daily budget to exit the learning phase within about a week (Meta's own guidance: roughly 50 optimization events per week per ad set)
  6. Turn on Dynamic Creative (4.4), upload 3-5 variants per asset type
  7. Launch, leave untouched for at least 3-4 days (the "learning phase") before judging or editing

4.6 — Optimization cadence

Daily for the first week (observe only), then weekly: kill anything above 1.5-2x your target cost-per-result with enough spend to be conclusive, scale winners by 20-30% every 3-4 days (not overnight — large jumps reset the learning phase), refresh creative every 2-4 weeks.

Part 5 — Your 30-Day Launch Plan

Week 1 — Foundation

  • Install and test all three pixels (Meta, TikTok, Google Tag)
  • Build your lead magnet / first offer page and confirm the conversion event fires correctly
  • Film/produce 3-5 creative variants per platform (a single message, reframed natively for each platform's format)

Week 2 — Cold launch

  • Launch cold campaigns on all three platforms simultaneously, modest budgets (60% of total budget split roughly evenly across platforms)
  • Do not touch bids/targeting for the first 3-4 days on each platform — let the learning phase complete

Week 3 — Build retargeting

  • By now you should have enough warm-audience volume (site visitors, video viewers, engagers) to launch retargeting campaigns (Part 1.1, Stage 2) on each platform
  • Shift budget toward whichever platform is showing the lowest cost-per-result so far

Week 4 — Scale and refresh

  • Scale winning ad sets/campaigns by 20-30% every 3-4 days
  • Kill anything that never found efficiency despite a full, undisturbed learning phase
  • Refresh creative across all platforms — by week 4 your week-1 creative is starting to fatigue
Realistic benchmark ranges (these vary enormously by niche/offer price — use as a rough sanity check, not a guarantee): Click-through rate 0.5-2%, cost-per-click $0.50-$3, cost-per-lead $5-$50 depending on offer value. If you're wildly outside these ranges in either direction after a full learning phase, the issue is almost always the offer or the hook — not the targeting settings.
