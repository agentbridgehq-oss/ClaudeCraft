# Digital Product Launch Checklist — Step by Step, End to End

A real checklist for shipping a digital product — the steps most first launches skip, with a Claude prompt for each one that benefits from it.

Email **support@claudecraft.ca** if you get stuck.

---

## Phase 1: Before You Build

- [ ] **Validate the pain point is real**, not assumed. `Prompt: I'm considering building [product idea] for [audience]. List the 5 strongest reasons this might NOT be a real enough problem to pay for, before I list reasons it might.`
- [ ] **Define the ONE person you're building for** — not "everyone interested in X."
- [ ] **Price it before you build it**, based on comparable products, not a round number that feels nice.
- [ ] **Write the sales page headline first.** If you can't write a compelling one before building, the offer likely isn't clear enough yet.

## Phase 2: Building

- [ ] **Scope to the smallest version that's genuinely useful**, not the most complete version you can imagine.
- [ ] **Name it clearly** — what it is and who it's for, not a clever name that requires explanation.
- [ ] `Prompt: Review this product description for clarity: [paste it]. Would a stranger understand exactly what they're getting and who it's for, in one read?`

## Phase 3: Pre-Launch

- [ ] **Write the FAQ before launch, not after the first confused email.** `Prompt: Based on this product description [paste it], list the 8 questions a skeptical buyer would have before purchasing.`
- [ ] **Set up a real refund policy and say it plainly** — ambiguity here costs more trust than a clear, generous policy.
- [ ] **Decide the launch price vs. the long-term price**, and whether there's a real deadline (don't fake urgency with no real cutoff).
- [ ] **Build the email sequence**: announcement, one value-first follow-up, one deadline reminder if there's a real one.

## Phase 4: Launch Day

- [ ] **Publish, then verify the actual buyer flow yourself** — pay (or fully test) your own checkout before telling anyone it's live.
- [ ] **Have the confirmation email ready and tested** — this is the first thing a real buyer sees; broken delivery here loses trust immediately.
- [ ] **Post where the people who already trust you are** — existing list/audience first, cold traffic second.

## Phase 5: Post-Launch (the part most launches skip entirely)

- [ ] **Read every piece of feedback from the first 10 buyers** — patterns here matter more than any single opinion.
- [ ] **Fix the most-asked question by updating the product itself**, not just answering it repeatedly in support.
- [ ] **Ask happy buyers for a real testimonial — and only use it if it's real.** Fabricated reviews are a genuine legal risk (FTC Consumer Review Rule), not just a bad look.
- [ ] **Set a recurring reminder to revisit pricing** after the first real sales data comes in — most first prices are wrong in one direction or the other.
