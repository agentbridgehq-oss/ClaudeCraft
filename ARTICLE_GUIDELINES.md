# ClaudeCraft Article Guidelines

This file is the single source of truth for what goes into ClaudeCraft articles.
`openclaw/daily-task.mjs` must follow these rules for every article it drafts or publishes.
Ken vets every article before it goes live — openclaw drafts, Ken approves, then publishes.

---

## What ClaudeCraft articles ARE

Strictly AI and Claude topics. Nothing else. Every article must fit one of these categories:

- **Claude / Anthropic updates** — new model releases, feature launches, pricing changes, safety announcements, API updates
- **Broader AI industry news** — competitor model releases (GPT, Gemini, Llama, etc.), AI policy and regulation, notable AI research papers or tools, significant industry shifts
- **Practical Claude guides** — prompt techniques, new Claude features explained for everyday users, tips that help readers get more out of Claude today

If a story doesn't clearly fit one of the three above, don't write it.

---

## What ClaudeCraft articles are NOT

Never write about any of the following, regardless of how it came up:

- Anything unrelated to AI or Claude (people, brands, jobs, industries, events outside AI)
- Vague or speculative AI stories with no real sourced development to report on
- Press-release rewrites with no added insight or value for the reader
- Anything that can't be sourced to a real, reachable, named publication or official announcement

---

## Required structure for every article

1. **Headline** — under 12 words, specific, makes someone want to click. No generic openers ("In a recent development…"). No reasoning leaks ("I'll write about…").
2. **Opening line** — a specific concrete detail, not a summary or context-setting sentence. Drop the reader into the story immediately.
3. **Body** — 300–450 words. Active voice. Short punchy sentences mixed with longer ones. Give an honest take on what it means for the reader, not just what happened. If it's a new feature, say what someone can actually *do* with it today.
4. **ClaudeCraft mention** — one natural, low-pressure closing line linking to `https://claudecraft.ca` or a specific bundle when genuinely relevant. Never a hard sell. Never omitted.
5. **Sources section** — real, named sources with clickable URLs. Every cited fact must trace back to a source listed here. No source = cut the claim.

---

## Source rules (non-negotiable)

- Every factual claim must have a real source
- Sources must be named publications or official Anthropic announcements — no anonymous blogs, no AI-generated summaries passed off as journalism
- Source URLs must be verified reachable before the article publishes
- No fabricated quotes, dates, statistics, or product names — ever
- If a story can't be sourced, don't write it. Skipping a day is better than publishing something unverified.

---

## Tone rules

- Write like a sharp, knowledgeable friend who follows AI closely — not a press release, not a generic blog
- Confident and opinionated: tell the reader what matters and why, don't just describe what happened
- Never hypey, never spammy, never "AI slop"
- Avoid: "In conclusion", "It's worth noting", "As we can see", "In a world where AI is changing everything"

---

## Review process (current)

Ken vets every article before it goes live. openclaw drafts; it does not auto-publish until Ken has approved the guidelines and the review step is removed or automated.

**Until further notice: openclaw should draft articles and hold them for review rather than auto-publishing.**

## Topics to search for (openclaw search guidance)

When searching for the Claude/Anthropic article:
- `Claude [current month year] new features`
- `Anthropic announcement [current month year]`
- `Claude model update [current month year]`

When searching for the broader AI article:
- `AI news [current month year]`
- `[GPT / Gemini / Llama / AI] release [current month year]`
- `AI regulation policy [current month year]`

Always prefer stories from the last 3–7 days. If nothing genuinely new is found, say so — do not recycle old news or invent a story.

---

## Topics to never search for or write about

- Anything outside AI / technology
- Specific people unrelated to AI (celebrities, athletes, politicians not involved in AI policy)
- Brands, products, or industries unrelated to AI
- Any topic Ken has explicitly ruled out in `CLAUDE.md`
