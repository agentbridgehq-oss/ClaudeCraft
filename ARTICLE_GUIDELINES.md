# ClaudeCraft Article Guidelines

The single source of truth for every article openclaw writes and publishes.
All rules here are non-negotiable. openclaw embeds these directly in its article prompts.

---

## Topic scope — strictly AI and Claude, always trending

Every article must be one of:
- **Claude / Anthropic news** — new model releases, feature launches, pricing changes, safety updates, API changes, product announcements
- **AI industry news** — competitor model releases (GPT, Gemini, Llama, Mistral, etc.), AI regulation and policy, notable research breakthroughs, significant tool launches
- **Practical Claude guides** — prompt techniques, feature walkthroughs, tips that help readers do something useful with Claude today

If a story doesn't fit one of those three, don't write it.

**Always pick the most current, trending story** — the thing people in AI are actually talking about right now. Check recency first: a 3-day-old story beats a 3-week-old story every time. If nothing genuinely new is found, say so — never recycle old news or invent a story.

**Never write about:** anything outside AI/Claude, Hurley or any other unrelated project, vague speculative pieces with no sourced development, press-release rewrites with no added reader value.

---

## Marketing rules — what makes a ClaudeCraft article work

ClaudeCraft's readers are everyday Claude users and AI-curious people. They're not researchers or developers. They want to know: *does this affect me, and what can I actually do with it?*

Every article must nail all four of these:

### 1. Headline that earns the click
- Under 12 words
- Specific and concrete — name the thing, not just "AI gets an update"
- Creates genuine curiosity or signals clear value: "What X Means for You", "Why X Matters", "X Just Changed — Here's What's Different"
- Never vague, never clickbait that doesn't deliver, never a reasoning-leak ("I'll write about…")

### 2. Opening that hooks immediately
- First sentence = the most interesting or surprising thing about the story
- Drop the reader straight into it — no "In a recent development…", no context-setting warmup
- If there's a specific detail, date, number, or moment that captures the story, lead with that

### 3. Body that gives real value
- 300–450 words
- Active voice, confident tone — write like a sharp, well-informed friend, not a press release
- Tell the reader what it *means*, not just what happened
- If it's a new feature or model: be concrete about what someone can do with it TODAY
- Opinionated but fair — if something is overhyped, say so; if it's genuinely exciting, say why
- Short punchy sentences mixed with longer ones — never a wall of text
- Avoid: "It's worth noting", "As we can see", "In a world where AI…", "In conclusion"

### 4. ClaudeCraft tie-in that feels natural
- One closing line linking to `https://claudecraft.ca` or a specific bundle when genuinely relevant
- Framed as a genuine recommendation, never a hard sell: "If you want to get more out of [X], ClaudeCraft has…"
- If no specific bundle fits, link to `https://claudecraft.ca` generally
- This line is non-negotiable — every article ends with it

## Source rules

- Every factual claim needs a real, named source (publication or official announcement)
- Source URLs must be verified reachable before publishing
- No fabricated quotes, dates, statistics, or product names — ever
- If a claim can't be sourced, cut it
- End every article with a Sources section listing all cited URLs as clickable links
- Skipping a day beats publishing something unverified

---

## Search strategy — how to find the right story

**For the Claude/Anthropic article**, search in this order of priority:
1. `Claude [model name] release [current month year]`
2. `Anthropic announcement [current month year]`
3. `Claude new feature [current month year]`
4. `Anthropic news this week`

**For the broader AI article**, search in this order of priority:
1. `AI news trending [current month year]`
2. `[GPT / Gemini / Llama] new release [current month year]`
3. `AI regulation [current month year]`
4. `AI research breakthrough [current month year]`

Always check: is this story from the last 7 days? If not, keep searching for something newer.
If no story from the last 7 days is found after searching, state that clearly rather than using older news.

---

## What NOT to search for or include

- Anything unrelated to AI or Claude
- Hurley — completely separate project, zero connection to ClaudeCraft or any other app
- AI stories that are purely speculative with no real announcement or release behind them
- Stories already covered in the last 7 days of ClaudeCraft articles (check recent articles first)

## Review process

Ken reviews articles before they go live. openclaw drafts; publish only after review is confirmed.
Once quality is consistent across several test articles, review can be removed and auto-publish resumed.
