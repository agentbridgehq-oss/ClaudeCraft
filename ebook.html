Chapter 1 — Communication (5 prompts)

1The Instant Email Reply

Read this email and write a complete, professional reply. Get to the point in the first sentence, answer every question they asked, and end with a clear next step. Keep it under 150 words unless it genuinely needs more. [paste the email]

2The Hard Conversation Opener

I need to have a difficult conversation with [who] about [what]. Give me the exact opening line to start with, not a vague topic — the actual first sentence — plus how to handle the most likely pushback calmly.

3The Tone Translator

Rewrite this message to sound [more professional / warmer / more confident / more concise], without changing the actual content or meaning: [paste your message]

4The Meeting Recap

Here are my raw, messy notes from a meeting. Turn them into a clean recap: decisions made, action items with owners if mentioned, and open questions still unresolved. [paste your notes]

5The Polite "No"

I need to decline this request without burning the relationship. Write a polite, brief, firm "no" that leaves the door open for the future where appropriate: [paste what you're declining]

Chapter 2 — Thinking & Decisions (5 prompts)

6The Devil's Advocate

I'm leaning toward this decision: [describe it]. Argue the strongest possible case AGAINST it — not a strawman, the actual best counter-argument a smart skeptic would make.

7The Clarifying-Questions-First Prompt

Before you answer, ask me 3 clarifying questions about this request — then wait for my answers before responding: [describe your request]

8The "Explain Like I'm Smart But New To This" Prompt

Explain [topic] to me the way you'd explain it to a smart friend who has zero background in this specific area — no unexplained jargon, but don't talk down to me either.

9The Honest Gut-Check

I'm about to do this: [describe it]. Don't just validate me — give me an honest read on the biggest risk I might be underestimating.

10The Decision Matrix Builder

I'm choosing between these options: [list them]. Build me a simple comparison across the factors that actually matter for this decision, and give me your direct recommendation at the end, not just a neutral table.

Chapter 3 — Writing & Content (5 prompts)

11The Hook Generator

Give me 10 scroll-stopping opening lines for a post about [topic], for [platform]. Make them genuinely different from each other, not 10 variations of the same idea.

12The Voice Matcher

Here's a sample of my own writing: [paste a paragraph]. Write [new piece] in that exact same voice and style.

13The Ruthless Editor

Edit this for clarity and tightness. Cut every filler word and redundant phrase, but don't flatten my voice or style: [paste your text]

14The Outline-First Writer

Before writing the full piece, give me just a structural outline for [topic/piece]. I'll approve or adjust it before you write the full version.

15The Repurposer

Turn this one piece of content into 5 different formats: a tweet, a LinkedIn post, an Instagram caption, an email subject + opener, and a one-sentence summary. [paste your content]

Chapter 4 — Money & Life Admin (5 prompts)

16The Budget Snapshot

My monthly income is [X]. My expenses are: [list them]. Organize this into categories, show what % of income each takes, and tell me one realistic savings target based on what's actually left.

17The Subscription Auditor

Here's everything I'm subscribed to and what I pay: [list]. Total it up, then tell me honestly which ones to keep, downgrade, or cancel based on how often I actually use them.

18The "Can I Afford This?" Check

I want to buy [item] for [price]. My income is [X] and I have [savings/debt context]. Give me a direct answer — comfortable, doable with a plan, or not right now — and show the math.

19The Bill Negotiation Script

I want to negotiate my [bill type] down. I've been a customer for [time] and pay [amount]. Write me the exact words to say on the call, including a fallback ask if they say no the first time.

20The Daily Planner

Here's everything I need to do today: [list]. Organize it into a simple, calm order — by time or importance, whichever makes more sense — and flag anything time-sensitive.

Chapter 5 — Learning & Growth (5 prompts)

21The Study Guide Builder

Turn these notes into a study guide with key terms, a summary of each section, and 10 practice questions: [paste your notes]

22The Skill Gap Finder

I want to get better at [skill/role]. Based on where I currently am [describe honestly], tell me the 3 highest-leverage things to focus on next — not a generic list, specific to my actual gap.

23The Feedback Simulator

Read this and give me honest feedback as if you were [a mentor / a beta reader / an interviewer] — what's working, what's not, and one specific thing to improve: [paste your work]

24The Accountability Check-In

My goal is [goal] by [date]. Help me break it into a realistic weekly target, and when I check in with my progress, be honest with me about whether I'm on track.

25The "Teach Me Like a Beginner" Prompt

I am a complete beginner at [topic]. Explain the absolute fundamentals first, in the simplest possible terms, before anything advanced. Tell me what to learn first, second, third.
